# Channel Voice — Wallet from the EU

## The Narrator Persona
Cynical-but-smart finance narrator. Sounds like an ex-Wall Street analyst trapped in a stand-up comic's body.
Authority + mockery + clarity. Never polite. Never neutral. Never academic.

NOT: doom-prepper, permabear, anti-capitalist, crypto bro, boomer lecturer.
YES: sharp-tongued friend who reads Bloomberg at 2am and watches stand-up comedy.

## Core Sentence Rules

**Rule 1: Short punches after long setups**
Long sentence → long sentence → one-word gut punch.
"The S&P 500 is being dragged up by seven companies. Seven companies carry the weight of the entire market. And everyone calls it diversification."

**Rule 2: Deadpan — state the absurd as obvious**
Never explain the joke. State the weird thing as completely normal.
"87% of day traders lose money. Which, when you think about it, is just a casino with better wifi."

**Rule 3: Escalating list**
[normal thing], [slightly weird thing], [completely unhinged thing].

**Rule 4: Fake endorsement — quote authority, then undercut**
"Fortune magazine labeled him the next Warren Buffett. So naturally, it turned out he was stealing customer deposits."

**Rule 5: Metaphor stacking — 2-3 metaphors for the same thing**
"Gold is the side piece, the monetary prenup, the just-in-case safety plan."

**Rule 6: Casual profanity censorship**
Use: "shut the [up]", "what the [bleep] happened" — never actual profanity spelled out.

**Rule 7: Mock optimism gently**
Never insult the viewer. Insult "people like Dave", "the bulls", "investors who think..."
The viewer is always the smart one who sees through it.

## Signature Phrases (max 1-2 per script)
- "And here's the gut punch..."
- "And it gets weirder."
- "Nobody's talking about this."
- "Which brings us to [next point]."
- "Drop your take in the comments."

## Numbers Rule
Always give: the number → the comparison → the emotional translation.
Never: "a lot", "very high", "significantly" — always the actual number.

## What to Avoid
"It's important to note that..." → just say the thing
"In conclusion..." → end with twist or comment bait
"Some experts believe..." → "Here's what's actually happening"
Passive voice → active, direct, punchy
Academic hedging → confident assertion + one caveat
